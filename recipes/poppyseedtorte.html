Lemon Poppy Seed Torte

This is a traditional Ukrainian torte, made even better with a tart rasberry curd filling, and a white chocolate whipped cream frosting. (frosting should be made day before for best texture) This torte is a tad bit heavier than poppy seed bakes you usually see, and the honey in the recipe gives it a distinct flavor and a richer texture. Now that I've introduced you to this Ukrainian delight, let's get stuck in!

Ingredients

For Torte

  • 1 cup poppy seeds
  • 1 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1 cup honey (melted, let cool)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup coffee, cooled
  • 6 eggs, beaten until thick
  • 3 cups all purpose flour (unbleached)
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Dash of salt

Rasberry Curd Filling

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pint fresh raspberries (1/2 of 12 oz. package frozen raspberries, thawed)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1/3 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp fresh lemon juice

Garnish

  • Fresh raspberries
  • Lemon peelings

White Chocolate Whipped Frosting

  • 4 cups whipping cream (33-35% fat)
  • 2 bars of white baking chocolate (white chips WILL not work)

Instructions

Torte

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F/ 148°C
  2. Combine granulated sugar, honey, vegetable oil, cold coffee, lemon juice, & lemon zest.
  3. Beat egg until thick (like gravy), then add to honey mixture and beat again for 2 mins.
  4. Sift flour, baking soda, baking powder, poppy seeds, and salt. Gradually add flour mixture to egg mixture & stir thoroughly after each addition.
  5. Pour into greased round 8' pans. Fill pans 2/3 full and bake for 40-50 mins or until toothpick comes out clean.

Rasberry Curd Filling

  1. Melt buter in large pan over medium heat
  2. Add raspberries, egg yolks, sugar and salt, and cook. (mash the raspberries) Stir frequently at first, then constantly towards the end until thick. (abt ten mins)
  3. Pour mix through a coarse strainer over a bowl, and press down to get as much liquid out as possible.
  4. Cool to room temp; the curd will keep thickening as it cools. Stir in lemon juice to taste. Cover and refridgerate until ready to serve.

White Chocolate Frosting

  1. Add chopped white chocolate to bowl & heat in microwave at 50% power for 3 mins. Heat whipping cream in different bowl for 2-3 mins. (or til warm, at regular power)
  2. Remove from bowls and combine whipping cream with melted chocolate, and stir well. Continue to cook in microwave at 20 sec intervals (reg power) until they're incorporated. There should be absolutely no lumps in the white chocolate.
  3. Cool on counter, then pour into large mixing stand bowl (or just large bowl), then cover. Let stand in fridge for 24 hours.
  4. Once it's chilled, (should be soft) use whisk attachment on stand mixwe & whip on low speed. Gradually work up to high speed until it's light and fluffy, with stiff peaks. (Will be stable in fridge for up to a week)
